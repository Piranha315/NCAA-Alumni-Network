Our Funding Model

Entirely member-supported. No strings attached.

We are funded entirely through voluntary member contributions and occasional event surpluses. We accept no corporate sponsorships, do not sell advertising, and are not affiliated with any university, athletic conference, or the NCAA.

Membership is and will remain free. This is a deliberate policy — we believe participation in alumni networks should not depend on financial means. As a result, we rely on members who are able to give back voluntarily to make programming possible for everyone.

The network is a registered informal association, not a 501(c)(3) charity. Donations are not tax-deductible. We are considering whether to pursue formal nonprofit status and will share that process with the membership if we do.

Our Commitments

What We Promise Our Members

These commitments hold regardless of how much is raised in a given year.

  • Publish a financial summary every January covering the prior year
  • Never sell member data or contact information to any third party
  • Seek member input before any single expenditure above $1,000
  • Maintain a reserve sufficient to cover at least six months of platform costs
  • Accept no money that comes with conditions on programming or membership criteria

Questions about our finances? Email the board directly.
