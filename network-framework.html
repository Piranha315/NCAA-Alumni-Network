Structure

How the Network Is Organized

Three tiers — national, regional, and local — coordinate seamlessly.

🏛️

National Board

A volunteer board of five alumni oversees overall direction, finances, and cross-chapter coordination. The board meets quarterly and publishes minutes publicly.

📍

Regional Chapters

Each city or metro area with five or more members may apply to establish a recognized chapter with its own elected chair and programming budget.

👤

Individual Members

All former collegiate varsity players may join. Members participate in the alumni directory, events calendar, and mentorship program.

Governance

How Decisions Get Made

Decisions affecting network-wide policy require a simple majority vote of chapter chairs. Financial decisions above $1,000 require board approval. We publish an annual financial summary available to all members.

Chapter chairs serve two-year terms and are elected by local members. Any member in good standing for at least one year is eligible to run. The national board is elected by chapter chairs.

We do not affiliate with any college, university, athletic conference, or the NCAA itself. Our use of the word "NCAA" in our name refers solely to the athletic association under which our members competed — we are an independent alumni collective.

Transparency

Open by Design

Board minutes and financial summaries are available to all members. Questions are always welcome.
